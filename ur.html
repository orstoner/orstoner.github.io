Hierarchical Framework

In 2017, now investigating under-reporting of tuberculosis in Brazil, I came up with a general hierarchical framework for correcting under-reporting in count data. Set in a Bayesian setting, the framework estimates the under-reporting mechanism and provides predictive inference for the unobserved true counts, both of which may depend on covariates.

\[ z_i \mid y_i \sim \mbox{Binomial}(\pi_i,y_i)\] \[ \log \left( \frac{\pi_i}{1-\pi_i} \right) = \beta_0 + \sum_{j=1}^J \beta_j w_{i,j} \] \[ y_i \sim \mbox{Poisson}(\lambda_i) \]

\[ \log(\lambda_i) = \alpha_0 + \sum_{k=1}^K \alpha_k x_{i,k} \]

Where covariates \(\boldsymbol{x}\) are related to the true count and \(\boldsymbol{w}\) are related to the under-reporting mechanism. The probability \(\pi_i \in (0,1)\) is interpreted as the reporting rate and \(\lambda_i\) is interpreted as the mean rate of the true count. Both models can also include simple or spatio-temporally structured random effects, such as the spatial effect (shown below) we include in our tuberculosis model to capture correlation in tuberculosis incidence between adjacent regions.
