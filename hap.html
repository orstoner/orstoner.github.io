Household Air Pollution

Since 2017 I have been working with Prof. Gavin Shaddick in collaboration with the World Health Organisation to develop a model for the use of polluting fuels for cooking.

Motivation

Approximately half of all households in low and middle income countries rely on polluting fuels. Which fuel a household primarily relies on for cooking is a key indicator for their exposure to household air pollution, which is estimated to globally kill millions each year.

Information on which fuels households rely on in each country is available in the form of national surveys, which are collated by the WHO in the Household Energy Database. However, surveys are never perfect, suffering from sampling variability, biases, missing information for some fuels and are at the very least limited by how frequently they are conducted in each country. To address this, we have developed a multivariate hierarchical model which allows us to learn about how the use of different fuels changes over time in the context of imperfect information and, importantly, robustly quantify the associated uncertainty.
